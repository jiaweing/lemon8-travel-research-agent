Here are the five types of investing that you can consider. For myself, I mainly invest in stocks and ETFs, and I also own a private property in Singapore for investment purposes.

1. Stocks üìà
Stocks represent a share of ownership in a company. Buying a stock means buying a piece of that company, which gives the investor a stake in the company's success.
üòäPros: The potential benefits of investing in stocks include the possibility of high returns over time. Stocks also offer liquidity, meaning they can be bought and sold easily and quickly.
ü´§Cons: Investing in stocks comes with risks. The value of your investment can fluctuate depending on the company's performance and market conditions. It is possible to lose money if the company performs poorly.
2. Mutual Funds üí∞
Mutual funds represent a pool of money gathered from numerous investors to invest in a diversified range of assets, like stocks and bonds. It's a way to spread investments across multiple assets to reduce risk.
üòäPros: The benefits of investing in mutual funds include diversification, which can help reduce risk by spreading investments across multiple assets. Mutual funds are also managed by experienced fund managers, which can be beneficial for investors who lacks investing knowledge. 
ü´§Cons: The cons of investing in mutual funds include the management fees that are associated with them. These fees can potentially reduce the net returns over time. The fund return may also vary depends on the management‚Äôs capability. 
3. ETFs ü™∫
Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs) represent a collection of different assets, such as stocks or bonds, similar to mutual funds. The difference is that ETFs are traded like individual stocks on stock exchanges.
üòäPros: The benefits of investing in ETFs include diversification, as they spread the investment across a range of assets, reducing risk. ETFs also provide the ease of buying and selling on stock exchanges, offering more flexibility compared to some other types of funds.
ü´§ Cons: The cons of investing in ETFs include lower returns as compared to stock investing. Investors also can‚Äôt pick individual stock as the portfolio is predetermined. 
4. Bonds ü§ù
Bonds are loans made by investors to companies or governments. By purchasing a bond, the investor is essentially lending money to the issuer for a certain period at a fixed interest rate.
üòäPros: Bonds are generally considered to be lower risk than other types of investments. They also provide predictable income through interest payments, making them an attractive option for those seeking regular income from their investments.
ü´§ Cons: The risks of investing in bonds include inflation risk and credit risk. Inflation risk refers to the possibility that the bond's fixed interest rate might not keep up with inflation, while credit risk is the possibility that the issuer may default on their payments.

‚ÄçReal estate investing involves buying properties to earn a return on the investment either through rental income or the property's appreciation over time.
üòäPros: The benefits of investing in real estate include a potential for steady cash flow from rental income and property value appreciation over time.
ü´§ Cons: The cons of investing in real estate include huge capital needed, potential high operating costs, such as maintenance and repairs, and the risk of local market conditions affecting property values.
